# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Youth members and families of the Akany Neaskol association in Antananarivo, Madagascar: they come to find event schedules, read testimonials, and stay connected to their community. Local residents and donors discovering the association for the first time. Admin staff managing content through the CMS. All users are French-speaking; many are on mobile.

## Product Purpose

Akany Neaskol is a Christian youth association founded in 2010. The site is their digital home — a place to tell their story, celebrate their three pillars (Spiritual, Social, Cultural), and invite others into their community. Success means members feel seen and welcomed, and newcomers understand immediately why the association exists and what it stands for.

## Brand Personality

Warm, rooted, faithful.

Voice is personal and communal — not institutional. Tone is gentle confidence: this community knows who it is and doesn't need to shout. Content speaks to the heart before the head.

## Anti-references

Not evangelical mega-church slick. No gradient-heavy hero sections, no "transformation" stock photography, no high-production-value emptiness. Not corporate NGO brochure-ware either — no grid of impact statistics, no formal sans-serif authority. Not a generic French association template.

## Design Principles

1. **Community before broadcast.** The site is a gathering, not a performance. Design decisions should feel like opening a door, not presenting a stage.

2. **Rootedness over polish.** Authenticity outweighs sophistication. Texture, warmth, and grain beat sleek surfaces. When in doubt, choose the choice that feels more human.

3. **Three pillars, equal and distinct.** Spiritual, Social, and Cultural carry equal weight. Their color identities (lemon, flame, cream) must remain legible and never flattened into a single brand accent.

4. **Faith is quiet, not loud.** Spirituality here is personal and communal. Design should evoke presence — stillness, depth, belonging — not spectacle or urgency.

5. **French at home.** The site is unapologetically local. Design language should feel particular to this community, not adapted from an international template.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. Reduced-motion support is already in place. Maintain minimum 44px touch targets. Keep color contrast ratios at 4.5:1 for body text. Ensure all interactive elements have visible focus indicators. No information conveyed by color alone (the three-pillar system uses color + label together).
